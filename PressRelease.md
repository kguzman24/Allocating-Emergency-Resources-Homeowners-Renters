
# Renters Left Behind After Floods!

## Same Storm, Half the Help
After Hurricane Helene, FEMA sent homeowners and averate of \$4,230 in housing aid; Renters received \$1,693.

## The Problem
FEMA's housing assistance program was built around property ownership. Renters, who may be disproportionately low-income, elderly, and from marginalized communities face proof of occupancy requirements and landlord documentation that may systematically block or reduce their rewards. After Helene, the gap left thousands of the most vulnerable households with significantly less aid than homeowners.

## The Solution
There must be a data-driven allocation tool that can identify high-vulnerability ZIP codes before aid is distributed. This way, emergency managers can prioritize outreach and help simplify documentation requirements. This would ensure that funding will be directed where it's needed most. We must prioritize those who need help; close the gap.
